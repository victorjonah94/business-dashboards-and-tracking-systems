Problems We Solve

Business Pain Points & What We Build to Fix Them

These are the exact challenges our clients come with. If any sound familiar, we can build a system that eliminates them.

01 P&L Visibility

I don't know if my business made profit last month.

We build a P&L dashboard that auto-calculates your net income, gross margin, and profit vs goal — updated in real time as your team logs entries.

02 Expense Leaks

Money is going out but I can't pinpoint where it's leaking.

We build an expense categorisation system with budget-vs-actual variance alerts so you see exactly where overspending is happening, by category and by week.

03 Data Chaos

My team tracks things in WhatsApp groups and four different spreadsheets.

We consolidate all data entry into one structured system with clear tabs, input validation, and a dashboard summary — so everyone records in one place.

04 Sales Pipeline

I have no visibility on my sales pipeline or who is buying what.

We build a sales CRM with lead source tracking, deal stage pipeline, product/service breakdown, and revenue by team member — so you know exactly what's converting.

05 Inventory Control

I have inventory but no idea what's available, what's sold, or what to reorder.

We build an inventory tracking system with available stock by location and SKU, sold units, reorder alerts, markup tracking, and payment status — like the N Solar dashboard in our portfolio.

06 Reporting Time

Preparing weekly reports takes my team hours every Friday.

We restructure data entry so reports auto-generate. The dashboard updates as entries are logged. Weekly review meetings become 20 minutes instead of two hours of data gathering.

07 Portfolio Returns

I have investment capital deployed across different assets but can't see my total returns.

We build a portfolio intelligence dashboard tracking each investment's maturity date, interest rate, compounded returns, and profit/loss — consolidated into one clear overview across all asset classes.

08 Budget Overruns

Project or event budgets always run over and I only find out at the end.

We build a budget tracking system with per-category spending limits, payment due dates, actual vs estimated comparisons, and automatic red-flag highlighting when a category goes over budget.
